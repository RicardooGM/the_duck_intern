The Thinkers

The minds that built the field.

Every model, every framework, every idea you'll encounter in finance and economics was built by someone. Here are the people worth knowing — and why they matter.

Profiles20+ thinkers
FieldsFinance, Econ, Behavioral
LevelAll levels
Last revisedApr 2026
"If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants." — Isaac Newton

Finance

Valuation, markets & investing
1894 – 197610 min

Benjamin Graham

The father of value investing. Taught the world that price and value are not the same thing — and built the framework that Warren Buffett still uses.

Value Investing
1957 – present10 min

Aswath Damodaran

The dean of valuation — making DCF accessible to everyone with a spreadsheet and a question. His datasets and models are used by analysts worldwide.

Valuation
1927 – 202310 min

Harry Markowitz

Nobel laureate who proved mathematically that diversification reduces risk. His efficient frontier changed how the world thinks about portfolios.

Portfolio Theory
1939 – present10 min

Eugene Fama

Father of the Efficient Market Hypothesis. The idea that you can't consistently beat the market — and the evidence that keeps proving him right.

Efficient Markets
1918–2003 / 1923–200012 min

Modigliani & Miller

Together they proved capital structure is irrelevant in a perfect market — and spent decades explaining why it very much isn't in the real one.

Corporate Finance
1934 – present10 min

William Sharpe

Developer of CAPM — the model that connects risk and expected return. His Sharpe Ratio remains the standard measure of risk-adjusted performance.

CAPM & Risk

Economics

Macro, micro & development
1723 – 179010 min

Adam Smith

The moral philosopher who gave us the invisible hand — and then warned us about the people it was attached to. The Wealth of Nations changed everything.

Classical Economics
1883 – 194611 min

John Maynard Keynes

Argued governments should run deficits in slumps. Politicians agreed during slumps; forgot during booms. His ideas still dominate fiscal policy debates.

Macroeconomics
1912 – 200611 min

Milton Friedman

Inflation is always and everywhere a monetary phenomenon. Champion of free markets and the architect of how central banks think about money supply.

Monetarism
1899 – 199211 min

Friedrich Hayek

The price system as information processor — why central planning can never match the distributed intelligence of free markets.

Austrian Economics
1915 – 200910 min

Paul Samuelson

The man who mathematized economics. His textbook defined how three generations of economists were trained — for better and for worse.

Modern Economics
1928 – 201510 min

John F. Nash Jr.

One equation, one equilibrium, one profoundly unstable world that now can't stop computing itself. Nash equilibrium is everywhere once you see it.

Game Theory

Behavioral & Psychology

How humans actually decide
1934 – 202411 min

Daniel Kahneman

Nobel laureate who showed that the rational agent of economics was, at best, a part-time job we took on reluctantly. Thinking, Fast and Slow changed everything.

Behavioral Economics
1937 – 199610 min

Amos Tversky

Kahneman's collaborator and the co-architect of Prospect Theory. Would have shared the Nobel Prize had he lived. The quiet genius behind the revolution.

Behavioral Economics
1945 – present10 min

Richard Thaler

Built behavioral economics into real policy. Nudge theory, mental accounting and the Save More Tomorrow program changed how governments design choices.

Nudge Theory
1946 – present11 min

Robert Shiller

Predicted the dot-com crash and the housing bubble. Proved that markets are driven as much by narrative as by fundamentals — and that CAPE ratios matter.

Irrational Markets
